About Me

Hi! I'm Ada, a PhD Candidate in the ASPIRE Group at The Ohio State University, advised by Prof. Donald S. Williamson. My research focuses on AI explainability and how it compares to human perception, particularly for sound and speech quality and intelligibility assessment. I believe that with the rapid growth of the AI field, we tend to focus too much on refining architectures or loss functions to gain minimal improvements without first understanding the root problem. In sound and speech assessment, the ultimate goal is match human perception, so my work aims to understand whether human perception and neural network predictions focus on the same decision-making factors.

Prior to my work on AI explainability, I worked with Prof. Michael D. Bond at The Ohio State University on the development of a framework for static and dynamic information flow control in the Rust programming language. I also earned an a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science & Engineering with a minor in Mathematics at The Ohio State University. I've been a Buckeye for 10 years and counting!
